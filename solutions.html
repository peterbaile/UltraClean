Problem


Sustainability becomes more and more important in today's life as we try to preserve our earth from the possible future of energy crisis. Yet, when we look around in our home, it is not hard to find that liquid detergents we use in both washing clothes and washing dishes pose huge damage to the environment. To acquatic lives, liquid detergents can destroy external mucus layers that protect the fish from bacteria and parasites as well as causing severe damage to gills. Also, phosphates present in detergents can lead to the explosive growth of algae which uses up the oxygen available for aquatic life. Pollution due to the widespread use of detergents has been an issue left untackled for a long time.

Our Solutions


Motivated by this problem, UltraClean tries to shape a future without the use of detergents at all. After months of research, we discovered the possibility of using ultrasound in achieving this aim. However, even ultrasound has been widely used in different fields, including diagnosis in medical field, cleaning of mechanical instruments in industries, and washing household items, such as spectacles and kitchenware, it has never been applied to a ultrasound washing machine.

Therefore, what UltraClean does is to attach transducers (equipments for transmitting ultrasounds) to the side of the washing tank. Thus, when the ultrasounds emitted by the transducers hit the fabrics on the clothes, it will explode at a very small scale and burst the stains away, thereby achieving the cleaning effect.
